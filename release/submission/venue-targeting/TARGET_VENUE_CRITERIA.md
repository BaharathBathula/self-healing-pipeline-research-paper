# Target Venue Evaluation Criteria

## Objective

Evaluate journals or conferences for the self-healing data pipeline paper before submission.

## Scoring Model

Use a 100-point score.

| Criterion | Weight | What to Check |
|---|---:|---|
| Scope fit | 30 | Does the venue publish work on data engineering, reliability, data quality, pipeline automation, MLOps, or distributed systems? |
| Contribution fit | 20 | Does the paper match the venue expectation: research contribution, system framework, artifact, empirical evaluation, or applied engineering? |
| Format/page fit | 15 | Does the current 8-page IEEE format satisfy the venue requirements? |
| Review and publication timeline | 10 | Does the timeline align with the user goal? |
| Visibility/indexing | 10 | Is the venue visible and credible for research evidence? |
| Cost/APC risk | 5 | Are publication charges acceptable or avoidable? |
| Artifact policy | 5 | Does the venue allow code, datasets, supplementary ZIP files, or reproducibility artifacts? |
| Rejection/desk-reject risk | 5 | Is the paper likely to be desk rejected due to scope, format, novelty, or article type mismatch? |

## Decision Rule

- 85–100: Strong target
- 70–84: Possible target
- 55–69: Backup target only
- Below 55: Do not submit

## Non-Negotiable Checks

Do not submit until these are verified from the current official venue page:

- Scope
- Article type
- Page limit
- Template requirements
- Anonymous vs non-anonymous submission
- Supplementary artifact rules
- Publication fees or APC
- Copyright/license rules
- Preprint policy
- Current deadline, if conference
