# Future Generation Computer Systems Verification Notes

## Venue

Future Generation Computer Systems

## Official Sources Checked

- https://www.elsevier.com/journals/future-generation-computer-systems/0167-739X/guide-for-authors
- https://www.elsevier.com/locate/future/

## Verified Requirements and Observations

Future Generation Computer Systems focuses on distributed systems, collaborative environments, high-performance computing, big data infrastructure, grids, clouds, and Internet of Things infrastructure.

Its scope includes complex workflow applications and big data processing/analysis on advanced computing infrastructures.

## Fit for Current Paper

Fit level: Medium, but high rejection risk in current form.

The self-healing data pipeline paper touches workflow reliability, data processing, automation, and infrastructure reliability. However, FGCS is a higher-bar systems venue and will likely expect a deeper systems contribution, stronger baselines, larger-scale experiments, and more rigorous evaluation.

## Risks

- Current 8-page paper is likely too lightweight for FGCS.
- Synthetic-only experiments may not be enough.
- Needs stronger comparison with distributed systems, workflow engines, observability systems, and recovery frameworks.
- Needs more detail on scalability, runtime overhead, fault model, and real-world deployment assumptions.
- Desk-reject risk is higher than IEEE Access or Journal of Big Data.

## Recommendation

Not the best first submission target.

Recommended next action:

Keep FGCS as a future stretch target after expanding experiments, adding baselines, strengthening systems contribution, and writing a full journal-length manuscript.
